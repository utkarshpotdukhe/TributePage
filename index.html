APJ Abdul Kalam

15 Oct 1931 - 27 July 2015

"Winners are not those who never fail but those who never quite"

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Early Life and Education

  • Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, full name Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam, was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India.
  • He came from a modest background and faced financial challenges during his childhood.
  • He graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli
  • later completed his Aerospace Engineering degree from the Madras Institute of Technology.

Scientific Career

  • Dr. Kalam joined India's space agency, ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation), and contributed significantly to the development of India's first indigenous satellite launch vehicle, SLV-III. He played a key role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998 and was known as the "Missile Man of India" for his work in the development of ballistic missile technology, particularly the Agni and Prithvi missiles.

Presidential Tenure

He served as the President of India from 2002 to 2007, becoming the "People's President." His presidency was marked by his vision of transforming India into a developed nation by 2020, known as the "Vision 2020."

Post-Presidential Activities

After his term as President, Dr. Kalam continued to be an inspirational figure for the youth. He was a prolific writer and authored several books, including "Wings of Fire" and "Ignited Minds." He actively engaged in educational initiatives and interacted with students to inspire them to pursue careers in science and technology.

Legacy

Dr. Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. His life and work continue to inspire generations of Indians and people worldwide, particularly in the fields of science, education, and national development.
